Frequently Asked Questions

Healthcare marketing questions answered

Common questions about working with a healthcare marketing specialist.

Healthcare marketing requires navigating HIPAA, state medical board rules, FTC advertising guidelines, and platform-specific policies (Facebook health restrictions, Google healthcare certification). General agencies get ads rejected, create compliance risks, and waste budget on ineffective tactics. We've handled these complexities for 200+ healthcare clients without a single compliance violation.

We serve diverse healthcare sectors: dentistry (general, cosmetic, orthodontics), medical aesthetics (med spas, plastic surgery), chiropractic care, mental health practices, optometry, dermatology, primary care, urgent care centers, specialty clinics, and multi-location medical groups. Each specialty requires distinct messaging, compliance considerations, and patient acquisition strategies.

Healthcare faces unique challenges: HIPAA-compliant lead capture, medical advertising regulations, managing online reputation in a review-sensitive industry, competing with large hospital systems, handling insurance versus cash-pay marketing, and building trust for sensitive health decisions. These nuances require specialized expertise general agencies lack.

We ensure compliance across multiple frameworks: HIPAA for patient data protection, FTC guidelines for testimonials and claims, state medical board advertising rules (which vary significantly), Google healthcare ad certification requirements, and platform-specific policies. Our legal review process catches issues before they become problems.

Reputation is critical in healthcare—patients read reviews before choosing providers. We implement HIPAA-compliant review generation systems, monitor and respond to reviews appropriately, address negative feedback strategically, and build your online presence across health-specific platforms like Healthgrades, Zocdoc, and Vitals alongside Google.

Yes. Multi-location groups require coordinated strategies: consistent branding with location-specific optimization, centralized lead routing, consolidated reporting, and budget allocation across locations. We've scaled healthcare marketing systems from single practices to 50+ location medical groups.

Marketing strategy differs significantly. Insurance-focused campaigns target volume and geographic coverage. Cash-pay marketing targets higher-income demographics, emphasizes value and outcomes over price, and focuses on elective procedures. Many practices want both—we create separate funnels for each patient type.

We track healthcare-specific KPIs: cost per patient acquired (not just leads), patient lifetime value by source, appointment show rates, treatment acceptance rates, and new patient revenue. These metrics matter more than vanity metrics like clicks or impressions. Our reporting ties marketing directly to practice revenue.

Healthcare sites need HIPAA-compliant forms, online scheduling integration, patient portal links, provider credentials prominently displayed, and trust-building elements (certifications, affiliations, testimonials with proper consent). Mobile optimization is critical—70%+ of healthcare searches happen on phones. We build sites that convert visitors into patients.

We integrate with healthcare-specific platforms: practice management systems (Dentrix, Open Dental, athenahealth), patient communication tools (Weave, Doctible, RevenueWell), online scheduling (Zocdoc, Acuity, Calendly with HIPAA compliance), and CRMs configured for HIPAA requirements. Data flows seamlessly while maintaining compliance.
